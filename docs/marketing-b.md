# Marketing direction B — "Everyone builds the product"

_2026-07-05. Alternative to [marketing.md](./marketing.md) (variant A: "design the product, not pictures"). Variant B leads with empowerment: AI lets the whole team contribute to the real product. "Not pictures" demoted to supporting point._

## The insight
Vibe coding proved designers and PMs *want* to build — they already do it on personal projects. Then they get to work and the real product is off-limits: logic scattered everywhere, mountains of setup, git rituals, changes they can't visualize, tools that need translation between roles. The barrier isn't desire or ability anymore — it's that enterprise codebases were built for one kind of contributor.

## Positioning (B)
**One-liner:** Everyone builds the product now.

**Category:** the shared workspace where the whole team — designers, PMs, engineers — works on the real product, with AI handling the translation to code.

**Emotional core:** empowerment + belonging. You're not commenting from the sidelines or handing off artifacts; you're contributing, safely, to the thing itself. Proper collaboration on the product — not collaboration on a picture of it (Figma) or a ticket about it (Jira).

## Narrative arc for the site (scroll order)
1. **Hero:** "Everyone builds the product now." Sub: you've vibe-coded your side projects — this is that power, on your company's real product, safely. Visual: one canvas, multiplayer cursors from different roles all editing live. CTA: *Connect your repo*.
2. **The wall (problem):** work software is hostile to non-engineers — scattered logic, setup, git, invisible changes, tool-to-tool translation. Short, felt, maybe illustrated as the "old pipeline" (Figma → ticket → handoff → wait).
3. **A surface for every role:** designer restyles the theme; writer edits copy in every language; PM flips a flag for a tenant and previews a funnel change — each in a bounded, visual surface. Nobody reads the codebase; everybody sees their change live.
4. **AI is the translator:** describe the change or just make it — the agent turns it into a reviewed pull request. You don't learn git; git happens. (Branches = drafts, PRs = proposals — the site should use plain language.)
5. **Real collaboration, one environment:** branch spaces at a URL, comments, review together — like multiplayer docs, but the doc is the product. Designers still round-trip to Figma when they want; it syncs both ways.
6. **Engineers set the boundaries:** one config defines what's editable; everything lands as a diff for review; code stays source of truth. Empowerment WITH guardrails, not instead of them.
7. **Where this goes:** background agents that watch your funnels and propose improvements; more surfaces (data models, contracts) — the whole team, building.
8. **Pricing** (same illustrative plans as A: Free hosted starter / Pro $25 w/ $15 credits / Team $40 / Enterprise; credit packs everywhere).
9. **Footer CTA:** "Stop waiting for the next sprint. Open your product."

## Voice & visual direction (B — deliberately distinct from A)
- People-first, warm, kinetic. A was product-journey/prism; **B is team-multiplayer energy**: role cursors (Figma-style named cursors — Maya · Design, Sam · PM, Rio · Eng) converging on one canvas, persona-driven vignettes.
- Interactive persona mechanic instead of A's parallax: e.g. an **"I'm a designer / PM / engineer" switcher** in the hero that re-personalizes examples down the page. (Idea, not strict requirement.)
- Warm saturated gradients, rounded/humanist type, hand-drawn-ish accents ok; still shows real product UI in vignettes — empowerment must look concrete, not abstract.

## Message discipline
- Never promise "no engineers needed" — the promise is *contribute safely*, engineers stay in control (that's a feature for both sides).
- "Not pictures" appears once, as support in section 5, never as headline.
- Vibe-coding referenced as cultural moment ("you've felt this power"), not as a competitor.
