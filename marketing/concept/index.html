screen 1 · overview

The whole app, at altitude 1

One structure — “Ferrytale”, an example SaaS — rendered as a derived structural map. Zones, flows between them, and the shared systems everything hangs off. Zoom is a first-class control. 2

Concept notes

  1. 1The map is derived from the codebase, never drawn. It can’t rot, and changing the map means changing the building.
  2. 2Altitude is one continuous surface: whole app → area → screen → element. Max zoom bottoms out in the workbench (screen 6), not an inspector.
  3. 3Shared systems (tokens, copy, flags, models) are drawn as structure — load-bearing members — not as settings pages.
  4. 4Flow arrows stay quiet by default; metrics appear when a lens asks for them (try the Product lens).
  5. 5Click any zone to drill in. The breadcrumb keeps altitude from ever being lost.
screen 2 · lens switcher

Same structure, four angles 2

Plan / elevation / section of one building. A lens re-annotates the map for a discipline — it dims the rest, it never hides it. 1 Lens and altitude compose: any angle at any zoom. 3

Concept notes

  1. 1Dim, never hide. Filter attention, not access — everything stays visible and clickable at 35% opacity.
  2. 2Same boxes, different labels. A lens changes annotation, not geometry — the views are all true, all of the same thing.
  3. 3Lens × altitude are orthogonal. Switch back to screen 1 — your lens and zoom persist; screens 1 and 2 are literally the same surface.
  4. 4▲n badges = changes visible through this lens this week. Zones with none recede; they never disappear.
screen 3 · change feed

Recent changes, at altitude 1

“The flooring will change.” One-liners at boundary level — skim past what doesn’t apply, expand to zoom to the affected spot. 2

Concept notes

  1. 1The feed speaks at boundary level — what changes about the building, not which files moved. Detail is one zoom away, never forced.
  2. 2Expanding a card is zooming: it opens onto the map spot it affects, not a separate detail page.
  3. 3One change, four readings: every expanded card summarizes the same underlying diff once per lens.
  4. 4The load-bearing card ignores lens filters. Clash detection: structural changes surface to every discipline by definition.
  5. 5Filtering dims rather than removes — same rule as the lenses. You always see that the other trades are working.
